Hello World! I'm Ujjwal Rimal

My name is Ujjwal Rimal, and right now, at 16 years old, my world feels like it's spinning forward – sometimes literally, on the wheels of my bicycle! Born on 2065/02/06 in the Bikram Sambat calendar (which is 19-05-2008 AD), my life took a really exciting and unexpected turn in 2024. That was the year I truly discovered two passions that now shape my days: cycle riding and vlogging. Before 2024, I was navigating school, friendships, and the usual teenage stuff. Life was good, but I hadn't quite found that thing that really sparked my energy. Then, something clicked. Maybe it was seeing others online, maybe it was a desire for more freedom, or maybe it was just the right time, but I got seriously into cycling. Exploring Nepal on two wheels opened up a new perspective. The wind in my face, the challenge of a hill, the simple joy of discovering a new path – it felt amazing. And almost as soon as I started riding more, I felt this urge to capture it, to share the experience. That's when vlogging entered the picture. In 2024, I also decided to start my YouTube channel, [Your YouTube Channel Name, if you want to include it, or just say "my vlogging channel"]. It felt like a natural combination. My bike could take me on adventures, and my camera could bring others along for the ride. Honestly, juggling school, cycling, filming, and editing is still something I'm figuring out. It's only been since 2024, so I'm definitely still learning! There have been shaky camera moments, editing frustrations, and rides where I felt totally exhausted. But every time I explore a new route, manage to capture a cool shot, or get a positive comment on a video, it fuels me to keep going. Being 16 means figuring out who you are and what you love. For me, right now, a big part of that identity involves the handlebars of my bike and the record button on my camera. It's my way of exploring the world, pushing my limits, and connecting with others who share similar interests. I don't know exactly where this road (or bike path!) will lead, but I'm excited to find out. My goal is to keep improving – become a stronger rider, make better videos, and share more of my adventures.
